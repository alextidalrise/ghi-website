# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Affluent buyers, lifestyle investors, and aspirational browsers considering residential property on or near premier golf courses in southern Europe (Spain and Portugal, expanding globally). The audience spans the full purchase funnel:

- **Dreamers** arriving through search or social, not yet committed, drawn by the lifestyle promise. They need to be seduced, not sold to.
- **Active buyers** comparing specific areas and properties, ready to enquire. They need depth, credibility, and frictionless contact.
- **Investors** evaluating rental yield, relocation logistics, and market trust signals. They need data presented with the same calm authority as the editorial content.

The demographic skews older and affluent. They expect legibility, clear navigation, and an interface that respects their time and intelligence. They are not impressed by complexity; they are impressed by curation.

## Product Purpose

Golf Homes International is a curated portfolio of residential properties tied to world-class golf, presented as an editorial marketing site with property listings, location storytelling, and lead capture (HubSpot enquiry forms, WhatsApp CTAs). It is not a property search engine. Success looks like a visitor who arrives curious, stays because the content feels trustworthy and the experience feels considered, and leaves an enquiry because the brand earned their confidence.

## Brand Personality

**Calm. Exclusive. Authoritative.**

The voice is that of a knowledgeable insider, not a salesperson. It speaks with quiet confidence, never urgency. Copy is precise, never breathless. The tone respects the reader's sophistication without being cold or inaccessible. Think: a private advisor who knows the market intimately and presents options without pressure.

## Anti-references

- **Property portals** (Rightmove, Idealista, Zillow): noisy, cluttered, transactional. GHI is the opposite of a search engine.
- **Generic luxury real estate sites**: stock photography, gold gradients, "exclusive lifestyle" in every headline. The aesthetic of aspiration without substance.
- **Corporate/institutional**: impersonal, cold, overly formal. GHI should feel warm and human despite its authority.
- **Flashy/animated**: parallax overload, autoplay video, motion for motion's sake. Movement exists only to provide feedback or draw attention to state changes.

## Design Principles

1. **Curation over volume.** Every property, every page, every word is selected. The site should feel edited, not generated. Fewer listings presented beautifully outperform a comprehensive database.

2. **Earn trust through restraint.** Luxury is communicated by what's absent: no clutter, no noise, no desperation. Generous negative space, considered typography, and photography that carries the emotional weight.

3. **The journey is the product.** A dreamer reading a location guide and an active buyer on a property page are both having a brand experience. Every touchpoint should feel like the same private club, from the first scroll to the enquiry form.

4. **Two modes, one voice.** The site operates in two registers: editorial pages that sell the lifestyle (location guides, area storytelling, the homepage) and detailed property listings that serve serious buyers with real information (specifications, gallery, pricing, enquiry). Both modes share the same calm authority. The editorial layer seduces; the listing layer delivers. Neither apologises for the other.

5. **Expert, not salesperson.** The brand knows more than the buyer. It shares knowledge generously (buying guides, area expertise) and lets quality speak. No hard sells, no countdown timers, no artificial scarcity.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Pragmatic approach: prioritise readability and usability for the target demographic (older, affluent, potentially unfamiliar with complex web patterns). This means:

- High contrast text on all surfaces
- Generous text sizes, especially for body copy and navigation
- Straightforward, predictable navigation patterns
- Touch targets sized for comfort, not minimum compliance
- Reduced motion respected (`prefers-reduced-motion`)
- Alt text on all property and location imagery
- No formal WCAG compliance target, but AA contrast ratios treated as a practical floor
