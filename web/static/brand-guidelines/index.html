Brand Guidelines

A reference for anyone producing content, design, or marketing materials for GHI. Covers logo usage, colour palette, typography, spacing, and tone.

Version 1.0 — May 2026

01 — Logo

Logo Versions

The wordmark reads "Golf Homes" in Playfair Display Bold, with "INTERNATIONAL" letterspaced beneath. Two horizontal rules frame the subtitle. Use the version appropriate to the surface.

Primary: Green on white/light backgrounds. Reversed: White on green/dark backgrounds. Never place the green logo on a dark surface or the white logo on a light surface.

Minimum clear space = height of the "G" on all sides

Minimum Size

Do not reproduce the logo smaller than 120px wide on screen or 30mm wide in print. At small sizes the subtitle becomes illegible.

Font Loading

Both typefaces are loaded from Google Fonts. Playfair Display: Regular 400, Medium 500, SemiBold 600, Bold 700, plus Italic 400 and 600. Libre Franklin: Light 300, Regular 400, Medium 500, plus Italic 300 and 400.

Italic Usage

Playfair Display italic is reserved for select display moments — hero taglines and pull quotes. It has a calligraphic quality that adds editorial character at large sizes. Never use italic for body text.

06 — Usage Guidelines

Do & Don't

Do

  • Use generous negative space — it signals exclusivity
  • Let photography carry emotional weight
  • Use gold sparingly — only for earned emphasis
  • Keep all corners square (0 border-radius)
  • Use 1px borders to define cards — never shadows
  • Set prices in tabular numerals
  • Use italic only for display taglines and pull quotes

Don't

  • Add rounded corners to any element
  • Use drop shadows at rest
  • Use borders thicker than 1px
  • Use gold as a background for large surfaces
  • Crowd elements — nothing should look packed
  • Use decorative elements, gradients, or ornaments
  • Set body text in the serif or in italic
07 — Brand Tone

Voice & Mood

Mood: Walking into a quiet room in a private club. A leather portfolio is placed in front of you. Everything has been considered. Nothing competes for attention. You feel welcomed, not sold to.

Direction: Luxury / Refined with Editorial influence. Minimal decoration — typography, negative space, and photography do all the work.

Audience: Affluent buyers and lifestyle investors seeking property tied to world-class golf. Aspirational, not exclusively ultra-high-net-worth.

Tone: Calm exclusivity — like a private members' club, not a property search engine.
